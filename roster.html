Kickers

Jan Stenreud

✖

Jan Stenreud

Bio

1967-1979 Kansas City Chiefs, 1980-1983 Green Bay Packers, 1984-1985 Minnesota Vikings. Entered college on skiing scholarship, joined football team as senior. Was 1st pure placekicker to enter Hall of Fame. Helped usher in more accurate soccer-style FG kicking. Retired as NFL’s second-leading all-time scorer (1,699 points) with then-record 373 FG made. Kicked 3 FG in Super Bowl IV win with Chiefs. Made 6 Pro Bowls, T-2nd most by kicker in NFL history. 1st-team All-Pro, 1970. Member of NFL’s 75th Anniversary Team. Born November 26, 1942, in Fetsund, Norway.

Accomplishments

  1. 1x All Pro
  2. 6x Pro Bowl
  3. 1x Super Bowl Champion
  4. Inducted into HOF 1991

Adam Vinatieri

✖

Adam Vinatieri

Bio

1996-2005 New England Patriots, 2006-Present Indianapolis Colts. Signed with Patriots as undrafted free agent after becoming all-time leading scorer at South Dakota State (since broken). Owns NFL records in (regular season and playoffs) for most career points, field goals made. Only kicker in NFL history to score 1,000+ points for 2 different teams. Has won 4 Super Bowl titles in 5 appearances, kicking game-winning FG in SB XXXVI and XXXVIII for Patriots. Set NFL record with 44 straight FG made, 2015-2016 seasons. 3-time Pro Bowl and 1st-team All-Pro selection. Named to NFL All-Decade Team of 2000s. Born December 28, 1972, in Yankton, South Dakota.

Accomplishments

  1. 3x All Pro

  4. Inducted into HOF 1991

Ray Guy

✖

Ray Guy

Bio

1973-1986 Oakland/Los Angeles Raiders. Selected by Raiders in 1st round (23rd overall pick) of 1973 draft, the 1st punter ever picked in 1st round. Averaged under 40 yards only once in 14 NFL seasons (42.4 career gross average). Had only three of 1,049 punts blocked. Played in 7 AFC championship games, 3 Super Bowls (winning 3 titles with Raiders). Made 7 Pro Bowls, tied for most by punter in NFL history. 3-time 1st-team All-Pro, T-3rd most by punter all-time. Named to NFL All-Decade Team for 1970s. Member of NFL’s 75th Anniversary Team. Born December 22, 1949, in Swainsboro, Georgia.

Accomplishments

  1. 1x All Pro
  2. 6x Pro Bowl
  3. 1x Super Bowl Champion
  4. Inducted into HOF 1991

1979-1992 San Francisco 49ers, 1993-1994 Kansas City Chiefs. Selected by 49ers in 3rd round (82nd overall pick) of 1979 draft.
