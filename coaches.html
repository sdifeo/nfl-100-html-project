Bill Belichick

Bill Belichick

Accomplishments

  1. Super Bowl Champion 6x

Bio

1991-1995 Cleveland Browns, 2000-Current New England Patriots. Ranks 3rd all-time in coaching wins. Overall record of 292-134-0, including playoffs (entering 2019 season). 31 wins in postseason, most in NFL history. Won more Super Bowls (6) than anyone in NFL history, also appearing in more Super Bowls (9) than any head coach. Has posted winning seasons in every year since 2001. Named Coach of the Year in 2003, 2007, and 2010. Born April 16, 1952, in Nashville, Tenn.

Joe Gibbs

✖

Joe Gibbs

Accomplishments

  1. Super Bowl Champion 3x
  2. Inducted into HOF 1996

Bio

1981-1992 Washington Redskins. NFL Coach of Year (1982, 1983, 1991). Noted for motivational skills, work ethic. Had 124-60-0 regular season record, 16-5 in postseason. Won 1982 NFC title, 4 NFC East crowns. Won Super Bowls XVII, XXII, XXVI with 3 different quarterbacks. Lost Super Bowl XVIII to Raiders. Won 10 or more games 8 times. Assistant coach with Cardinals, Buccaneers, Chargers. Born November 25, 1940, in Mocksville, North Carolina.

Paul Brown

✖

Paul Brown

Accomplishments

  1. AAFC Champion 4x; NFL Champion 3x
  2. Inducted into HOF 1967

Bio

1946-1962 Cleveland Browns. Exceptionally successful coach at all levels of football. Organized Browns in AAFC, 1946. Built Cleveland dynasty with 222-112-9 record (including playoffs), 4 AAFC titles, 3 NFL crowns, only one losing season in 17 years. A revolutionary innovator with many coaching “firsts” to his credit. Elected to Pro Football Hall of Fame before Cincinnati Bengals tenure began. Born September 7, 1908 in Norwalk, Ohio. Died August 5, 1991, at age of 82.
